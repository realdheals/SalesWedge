Sales Wedges or, thoughtful ways to handle objections on sales calls

“Honesty and integrity are by far the most important assets of an entrepreneur.” - Zig Ziglar

1. Introductions

a) “Bob Speaking” “Bob, thanks for taking the call, we’ve never spoken before and you don’t know me. Is this a bad time?” “ahh no, make it quick!”

b) “Bob speaking” “Bob, my name is Danny, thanks for taking my call, we make x product which helps y part of your business. Do you look after that side of the business?” “Yes that would be me - or - no, but I can put you through to someone who does”

c) “Bob Speaking” “Bob, thanks for taking my call, very quickly, If in the next 93 seconds I could introduce you to a product that would help your business make more money would you give me the time.” “The clock starts now”

2.Pre Mature Price Requests

a)”Just tell me how much it is.”

“The price depends. What I can tell you though is that we’re only going to do business if we have a solution that fits your budget. Let me ask you a few questions. Do you currently have a budget for X? If you did buy X, what sort of results would you want from it”

3. Don’t Call Me

a) Sir, I’m sorry, it seems that I haven’t provided enough information for you to feel comfortable working with us? Am I close?

b) Sir, I’m sorry, have I done something to offend you?

4. Being Continually Rescheduled

a) “Sir, this is the 3rd time you’ve told me you’re busy. I think you want to learn more about how this product will solve your problems though, am I right? “Yeah I’m interested” “Look, let’s call an intervention. I need you to commit to listening to me for 5 minutes now (or a time in the near future) and if you’re not convinced you want to proceed we can call it quits. Fair?”
